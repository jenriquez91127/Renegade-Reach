# Renegade Reach — Audience Profile

## Primary Audience
BC students in distress or emotional difficulty — broadly defined.

## Who They Are
- Bakersfield College students (community college population)
- Often first-generation college students
- Many carry financial pressure (working while studying, supporting family)
- Navigating identity — cultural, generational, personal
- Academically stressed, often without strong support networks at home
- May have grown up in communities where mental health conversations are stigmatized
- Age range: broadly 17–30+, with significant adult learner population

## Their Barriers to Traditional Counseling
- Feels like a burden or weakness
- Logistically hard — appointments, wait times, location
- Culturally stigmatized in many BC student communities
- Fear of judgment or records
- Don't think their problem is "serious enough" to warrant help
- Simply don't have time

## What They Need From Ren
- Someone who gets it without needing a long explanation
- No judgment, no appointment, no waiting
- Permission to say what they're actually feeling
- A space that feels safe and private
- To be heard before being helped

## Design Implications
- The interface must feel warm and human — not clinical, not corporate
- Language must be student-proximate — not academic, not formal
- The experience of opening the page must already feel like a relief
- Accessibility matters: mobile-first, low bandwidth consideration, clear contrast
- Trust signals must be immediate and genuine — not marketing-speak
